Big Mac Crunchwrap

Description

If you love the iconic flavors of a Big Mac, this hearty Crunchwrap Casserole delivers everything you crave in a fun, family-style bake. Layers of seasoned beef, melty American cheese, crunchy tostadas, tangy pickles, and creamy special sauce are wrapped in tortillas and baked until golden and crispy.

Ingredients

Big Mac Sauce

  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons yellow mustard
  • 2 tablespoons sweet pickle relish
  • 2 tablespoons grated onion
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon sugar

Casserole

  • 1 1/2 pounds extra-lean ground beef (93% lean)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons diced minced onion
  • 6 (10-inch) flour tortillas
  • 12 slices American cheese
  • 1 (16-ounce) jar crinkle-cut dill pickles, drained (about 1 1/2 cups)
  • 4 tostada shells
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 1 cup shredded lettuce
  • 1/2 cup Big Mac sauce (see above), plus more for topping
  • Sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C)
  2. Lightly grease a 9x13-inch baking dish
  3. For Big Mac sauce, whisk together mayonnaise, mustard, relish, grated onion, vinegar, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, and sugar in a medium bowl until smooth
  4. Set aside Big Mac sauce
  5. Heat a large skillet over medium heat
  6. Add ground beef, garlic powder, and black pepper
  7. Cook, breaking up meat into crumbles, until browned, 7 to 8 minutes
  8. Drain excess grease; set aside
  9. Meanwhile, place dried minced onion in a small bowl and cover with warm water
  10. Let stand 5 minutes
  11. Drain well and set aside
  12. Arrange flour tortillas in the prepared baking dish, allowing them to hang halfway over the edges and fully cover the bottom of the baking dish
  13. Spread half of the cooked beef evenly over tortillas
  14. Sprinkle with half of the hydrated onions and top with 6 slices American cheese
  15. Arrange half of the pickle slices evenly over cheese
  16. Place tostada shells over pickles, breaking as needed to fit in an even layer
  17. Spread 1/2 cup sauce evenly over tostada shells
  18. Repeat layers once with remaining beef, onions, cheese, and pickles
  19. Fold flour tortillas over the top to fully enclose the filling
  20. Brush the top with melted butter
  21. Bake until tortillas are golden brown and crisp and filling is heated through, about 30 minutes
  22. Let stand for 5 minutes
  23. Place a large cutting board over the baking dish and carefully invert the casserole onto the cutting board
  24. Top with shredded lettuce, drizzle with some of the additional Big Mac sauce, and sprinkle with sesame seeds just before serving
  25. Serve with remaining Big Mac sauce
